SEASONS

SUMMER

Summer is the hottest of the four temperate seasons, occurring after spring and before autumn. At or centred on the summer solstice, daylight hours are longest and darkness hours are shortest, with day length decreasing as the season progresses after the solstice.

WINTER

The days are shorter and the nights get longer. The chilly mornings give you a different sense altogether. Hot drinks like coffee, tea, and hot chocolate are enjoyed more during winters. The sun rises quite late and sometimes it does not. Winter season can be characterized by cold wind blow, falling of snow, very low atmospheric temperature, short day, long night, etc. This season lasts about three months, starts from December and ends in March.

RAINY

The rainy season, also called the “wet season”, is the time of the year when a region's average rainfall occurs. In India, the rainy season starts in June and ends in September. Along with the looming clouds, the rainy season is characterized by high humidity and strong winds
